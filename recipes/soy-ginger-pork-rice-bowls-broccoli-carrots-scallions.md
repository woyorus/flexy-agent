These bowls are savory, gingery, and fresh, with tender pork, crisp vegetables, fluffy rice, and a sesame finish. They are designed for meal prep: light enough for lunch, but still filling and satisfying.

Equipment: medium saucepan, large skillet or wok, chopping board, knife, spatula, and airtight containers.

1. Cook the jasmine rice until tender, then fluff it and keep it warm.
2. Stir the soy sauce, ginger, and garlic together in a small bowl.
3. Heat the sesame oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Slice the pork into thin strips, then cook it until browned and just cooked through.
4. Add the broccoli and carrots, then stir-fry until crisp-tender. If the pan starts to dry out, add a splash of water.
5. Pour in the soy-ginger mixture and toss well. Fold in the scallions and cook for a final minute.
6. Divide the rice between containers or bowls, top with the pork and vegetables, and finish with the sesame seeds.

Storage: keep covered in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in the microwave until steaming hot; add a splash of water if needed.

Tip: For a little heat, add sliced fresh chili or a pinch of chili flakes. If you like a fresher finish, keep the scallions and sesame seeds separate until serving.
