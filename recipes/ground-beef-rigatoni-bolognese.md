This is a rich, meal-prep friendly bolognese built around ground beef, softened aromatics, and a short tomato simmer. Keeping the pasta separate makes the portion easy to scale and helps it stay firmer when reheated.

Prep time: about 45 minutes.
Equipment: large saute pan or wide pot, medium saucepan, colander, knife, chopping board, grater.

1. Finely chop the onion and carrot, mince the garlic, and grate the parmesan.
2. Heat the olive oil in a large saute pan over medium heat. Add the onion, carrot, and sea salt, and cook until softened and lightly sweet.
3. Add the garlic and dried oregano and cook briefly until fragrant.
4. Add the ground beef and black pepper. Cook, breaking the meat up well, until browned and no longer wet.
5. Stir in the tomato passata, bring to a gentle bubble, then lower the heat and simmer until the sauce is thick, rich, and glossy.
6. Meanwhile, cook the rigatoni or spaghetti in boiling water until just al dente, then drain well.
7. Serve the lean beef bolognese over the rigatoni or spaghetti and finish with the parmesan. For meal prep, pack the lean beef bolognese and the pasta separately so the pasta keeps a better texture.

Storage: Cool before sealing and refrigerate for up to 3 days. The sauce freezes very well, and for best texture it is better to freeze the lean beef bolognese separately from the pasta.
Reheating: Reheat until piping hot, loosening the sauce with a small splash of water if needed.
Tip: If you want to adjust calories later without changing the flavor balance of the meal, change the pasta first and leave the sauce the same.
