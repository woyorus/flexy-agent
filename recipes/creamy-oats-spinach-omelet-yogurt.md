A filling breakfast with creamy chia oats, a soft spinach omelet, and a cold yogurt bowl on the side. It keeps the oatmeal front and center while adding enough protein and fat to stay satisfying for hours.

Prep time: 10 minutes.
Equipment: small saucepan, non-stick frying pan, mixing bowl, whisk or fork, spatula, and a serving bowl.

1. In the saucepan, combine {rolled oats}, {chia seeds}, {water}, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat and cook for **3-4 min**, stirring often, until thick and creamy.
2. Let the oats stand for **1 min** so they thicken a little more while you finish the eggs.
3. Whisk {egg}, {liquid egg whites}, and {whole milk} in a bowl with another pinch of salt. Heat {olive oil} in the non-stick pan over medium heat for **30 sec**. Pour in the egg mixture, add {baby spinach}, and cook for **2-3 min** until the edges set. Fold the omelet and cook for **1 min** more.
4. Spoon {plain yogurt 3.2% fat} into a small bowl, dust with cinnamon, and serve it cold alongside the oats and omelet.

Storage: keep the oats and omelet refrigerated for up to 2 days, and store the yogurt separately. Reheat the oats with a splash of water for **60-90 sec** in the microwave and warm the omelet for **20-30 sec**; add the yogurt after reheating.
Tip: For a sweeter version, add strawberries or a few slices of banana on a different day and reduce the oats slightly next time to keep the macros similar. For a more savory version, skip the cinnamon and finish the omelet with black pepper.
