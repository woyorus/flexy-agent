A bright, meal-prep-friendly rice bowl with smoky chicken, black beans, sweetcorn, creamy avocado, and a fresh tomato-lime salsa. It reheats well and stays satisfying without feeling heavy.

Equipment: saucepan with lid, frying pan or skillet, chopping board, knife, and a mixing bowl.

1. Cook the basmati rice until tender, then keep it covered while you finish the rest.
2. Season the chicken breast with cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper.
3. Heat olive oil in a skillet and cook the chicken breast until golden and cooked through. Rest it briefly, then slice.
4. Add the black beans and sweetcorn kernels to the skillet and warm them through.
5. Mix the tomato, red onion, lime, fresh coriander, and a pinch of salt to make the salsa.
6. Build bowls with the rice, chicken, black beans, sweetcorn, avocado, and tomato-lime salsa.
7. For meal prep, cool the components before sealing. Store in the fridge for up to 3 days, and reheat the rice, chicken, black beans, and sweetcorn before adding the salsa and avocado. If you want a brighter finish, squeeze over a little extra lime right before eating.
