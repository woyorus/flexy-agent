Rustic beef ragù with mushrooms, tomato, red wine vinegar, and parmesan, served with crisp rosemary roast potatoes. It is hearty, comforting, and a clean break from bolognese while still scratching the same dinner itch.

Prep time: about 60 min. Equipment: large deep skillet, oven tray, mixing bowl, wooden spoon.

1. Heat the oven to 220°C (200°C fan). Toss the potatoes with {olive oil (for potatoes)}, salt, black pepper, and rosemary, then spread them out on a tray and roast for 35-40 min, turning halfway, until golden and crisp.
2. While the potatoes roast, heat {olive oil (for ragù)} in a large deep skillet over medium heat for 1 min. Add the {onion} with a pinch of salt and cook for 5-6 min until soft. Add the {button mushrooms} and cook for 6-7 min, stirring now and then, until they have released their water and started to brown.
3. Add the {garlic} and {tomato paste}, then stir for 1 min until the paste darkens slightly. Add the {ground beef (10% fat)} and break it up with a spoon. Cook for 6-8 min until nicely browned.
4. Pour in the {red wine vinegar} and let it bubble for 1-2 min, scraping up the bottom of the pan. Add the {ripe tomatoes}, season with salt, black pepper, and a little oregano, then simmer for 15-20 min until thick, glossy, and rich.
5. Turn off the heat and stir in the {parmesan} for 30 sec. Serve the ragù with the potatoes on the side and spoon the sauce over the top. If you want a looser sauce, add a splash of water during the simmer.

Storage: cool quickly, then store the ragù and potatoes separately in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 3 days. Freeze the ragù for up to 3 months; the potatoes are best fresh.
Reheating: reheat the ragù in a saucepan over medium heat for 4-5 min, or microwave for 2-3 min, until piping hot. Re-crisp the potatoes in a hot oven or air fryer at 220°C for 8-10 min; if you are in a rush, microwave them, but they will be softer.
Tip: use a wide pan so the beef browns instead of steaming, and do not skip the reduction step - that is what makes the sauce taste like a proper ragù.
