Bright Mediterranean tuna bowl with rice, chickpeas, and feta. It tastes fresh, salty, and lemony, but it is sturdy enough for a work lunch and reheats well.

Total time: about 45 minutes.
Equipment: saucepan with lid, colander, chopping board, knife, mixing bowl, and airtight containers.

1. Rinse the basmati rice and cook it in salted water until tender, then drain and let it steam-dry for a few minutes.
2. While the rice cooks, chop the cherry tomatoes, red bell pepper, and red onion, then slice the olives and parsley.
3. In a mixing bowl, combine the tuna, chickpeas, cherry tomatoes, red bell pepper, red onion, olives, feta, olive oil, lemon juice, and parsley.
4. Taste the tuna mixture and adjust with a little more salt or lemon if needed.
5. Divide the rice between containers and spoon the tuna salad on top.
6. Seal and refrigerate once cool. Keep for up to 3 days.
7. Reheat in the microwave until hot, then stir and add extra lemon if you want it brighter.

Tip: This also tastes good cold if you prefer a no-reheat lunch. For the best texture, drain the tuna and chickpeas well before mixing.
