Equipment: large pot, large skillet, colander, wooden spoon, tongs, knife, cutting board.

This creamy salmon and shrimp linguine has a Spanish coastal feel: silky lemon cream sauce, spinach, and a little parmesan, with just enough richness to feel special. It is simple enough for a weeknight and holds up well for meal prep.

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the linguine until just al dente. Reserve a little pasta water, then drain.
2. Pat the salmon dry and season with salt and black pepper.
3. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook the salmon until lightly browned and just cooked through, then break it into large flakes and set aside.
4. Add the onion and garlic to the same skillet and cook until softened and fragrant. Stir in the shrimp and cook until they just start to turn pink.
5. Pour in the water or fish stock if available, otherwise use water, and let it bubble for a minute. Stir in the cooking cream, baby spinach, lemon juice, and parmesan, then simmer until the sauce lightly coats the back of a spoon.
6. Return the salmon to the pan, add the linguine, and toss everything together. Use a splash of reserved pasta water if needed, then taste and adjust the seasoning.

Storage: cool quickly and refrigerate in airtight containers for up to 3 days. Reheat gently in a skillet or microwave with a splash of water; keep the heat low so the seafood stays tender. For best texture, store the pasta and sauce separately if you are meal prepping.

Tip: if you want a more seafood-forward version, swap part of the shrimp for calamari and cook it only until opaque. A pinch of chilli flakes also works well here.
