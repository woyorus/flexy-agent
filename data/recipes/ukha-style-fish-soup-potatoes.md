A light, clear fish soup inspired by Russian ukha, with tender cod, richer salmon, potatoes, and dill in a clean lemony broth. It is a practical lunch meal prep because it reheats well, stays comforting without feeling heavy, and the bread on the side makes it more filling.

Prep time: about 40 minutes.
Equipment: large pot, chopping board, sharp knife, ladle, and 2 meal-prep containers.

1. Heat {olive oil} in a large pot over medium heat for **1 min**. Add {yellow onion} and cook **4-5 min** until softened. Add {carrot, raw} and cook **2 min** more.
2. Stir in {potatoes, raw} and {water}. Bring to a boil over high heat for **6-8 min**, then reduce to a gentle simmer and cook **12-14 min** until the potatoes are almost tender.
3. Add {cod fillet, raw} and {salmon fillet, raw}. Simmer gently for **5-6 min** until the fish flakes easily. Turn off the heat, stir in {fresh dill} and {lemon juice}, then season with salt and black pepper.
4. Rest for **2 min**, then ladle into containers and serve with {rustic white bread} on the side, toasted if you like.

Storage: cool the soup quickly, then refrigerate it in a sealed container for up to 3 days. Keep the bread separate so it stays dry. Freezing is not ideal once the fish and potatoes are in the pot.
Reheating: microwave covered at medium power for **2-3 min**, stir, then heat **1-2 min** more until steaming; or warm gently in a saucepan over low heat for **4-5 min**. Do not boil hard or the fish will break apart.
Tip: if you want a more traditional finish, add an extra pinch of dill and a little black pepper right before serving.
