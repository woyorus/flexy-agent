Salmon and Calamari Linguine in Tomato-Cream Sauce
A rich, seaside-style pasta with seared salmon, tender calamari, and a silky tomato-cream sauce. It feels restaurant-style but is straightforward enough for a weeknight and reheats well for meal prep.
Prep time: 40 minutes.
Equipment: large pot, large skillet, colander, tongs, and a wooden spoon.

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook {dry linguine} until al dente, **9-10 min**. Reserve a small splash of pasta water before draining.
2. While the pasta cooks, heat {olive oil} in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add {raw salmon fillet} cut into 3-4 large chunks, season with salt and black pepper, and sear **1-2 min per side** until lightly golden. Transfer to a plate.
3. Lower the heat to medium. Add {yellow onion} and cook **4-5 min** until soft. Add {garlic cloves} and a pinch of chili flakes, then cook **30 sec** more.
4. Stir in {tomato passata} and {dry white wine}, then simmer **4-5 min** until slightly reduced. Pour in {cooking cream 18% fat} and simmer **2 min** more.
5. Add {cleaned raw calamari rings} and cook **2-3 min** until just tender and opaque. Return the salmon and any juices to the pan and warm through **1 min**. Stir in chopped parsley and a splash of reserved pasta water until the sauce turns glossy.
6. Toss the drained linguine through the sauce for **1 min** so every strand is coated. Taste and adjust with salt and black pepper, then serve immediately.

Storage: Cool quickly, portion into airtight containers, and refrigerate for up to 3 days. Not ideal for freezing because the pasta and calamari soften.
Reheating: Reheat gently in a skillet over medium-low heat for **3-4 min**, adding a splash of water if needed. The microwave also works in short bursts; stop as soon as it is hot so the calamari stays tender.
Tip: If you are meal-prepping this, cook the pasta 1 minute shy of al dente so it stays better after reheating.
