# Project Vision Document

## Working title

Flexie

## What this project is

This project is about building a low-friction AI-guided weight-loss system for people who repeatedly fail restrictive diets in real life.

It is not centered on nutrition perfection, food morality, or detailed tracking. It is centered on one core outcome: helping people lose weight and keep it off by maximizing adherence to a sustainable calorie deficit.

The project is based on the idea that most weight-loss approaches fail not because people do not know that calorie deficit matters, but because the method is too restrictive, too fragile, too annoying, and too hard to sustain when real life gets in the way.

This project exists to solve that.

---

## Core project thesis

Weight loss is not mainly a knowledge problem. It is an adherence problem.

People often know the basics:

* calorie deficit matters
* consistency matters
* protein is useful
* movement helps

What usually fails is not understanding.
What usually fails is execution over time.

Restrictive diets can work in the short term, but they often break under real-life conditions:

* cravings
* restaurants
* family meals
* weekends
* social eating
* mental fatigue from rules and tracking
* rebound after restriction

This project is built around the belief that the winning system is not the most aggressive one. It is the one that people can actually stay with long enough for results to compound and last.

---

## The project's purpose

The purpose of this project is to create a weight-loss system that is:

* highly adherent
* low friction
* psychologically livable
* flexible in real-life conditions
* planning-first rather than tracking-first
* focused on sustainable results rather than short-term restriction

The goal is not to build another dieting app.

The goal is to build a system that makes weight loss work in real life.

---

## Who this project is for

This project is for people who:

* have tried to lose weight before
* often succeed in the short term
* repeatedly rebound or restart
* are tired of restrictive diets
* are tired of obsessive tracking
* want to lose weight without giving up flexibility
* need something that works with restaurants, cravings, family meals, and unpredictable life

This project is especially for people who do not need another lecture on what calorie deficit is. They need a system that helps them live it consistently.

---

## The enemy

The project is built in opposition to:

### 1. Restrictive dieting

Too many rules. Too much restriction. Too much friction. Too much psychological strain.

### 2. Yo-yo dieting

Short-term success followed by rebound, regain, and repeated restarting.

### 3. Weight-loss systems that only work under ideal conditions

Plans that collapse the moment real life enters the picture.

### 4. Tracking-heavy approaches

Systems that make the user do too much manual work without actually solving the moments that usually break adherence.

---

## Core principles

### 1. Adherence is the main variable

The system should optimize for long-term adherence first.

Not maximum restriction.
Not perfect compliance.
Not idealized food behavior.

If the user cannot stick to it, it does not work.

### 2. Low friction comes first

Low friction is not just a UX preference. It is a core adherence principle.

The project should reduce:

* manual effort
* unnecessary logging
* repetitive decisions
* food math
* rule management
* recovery friction after off-plan moments

The project should make it easier to stay on track, not harder.

### 3. The system must be planning-first

Tracking after the fact is not enough.

The project should be centered on:

* planning what is likely to happen
* preparing for cravings, meals out, and social eating
* adapting quickly when something changes
* keeping the week on track

This is not primarily a tracker.
It is primarily a planning and adjustment system.

### 4. Flexibility is required

Flexibility is not a bonus feature. It is part of the method.

The project must allow for:

* desired foods
* fun foods
* eating out
* restaurant meals
* family meals
* unplanned events
* imperfect days

The system should bend without breaking.

### 5. Psychology matters

A plan that is technically correct but psychologically miserable is not a good plan.

The project should avoid:

* food demonization
* purity-based eating frameworks
* rigid meal-plan thinking
* punishment after deviations
* all-or-nothing responses

The system should help the user stay emotionally and behaviorally stable enough to continue.

### 6. Weekly control matters more than daily perfection

The project should emphasize the week more than the day.

A bad meal should not become a bad day.
A bad day should not become a bad week.
The system should help preserve weekly direction.

### 7. Real life is the main environment

Restaurants, cravings, weekends, family meals, takeout, and social plans are not edge cases.

They are the actual operating environment.

Any solution that only works at home, in ideal conditions, is not enough.

---

## Position on calorie deficit

This project is explicitly about weight loss, so calorie deficit remains fundamental.

However, the project does not treat weight loss as a matter of simply pushing food lower and lower.

The intended approach is:

* keep food intake as high and as livable as possible
* keep the calorie deficit sustainable
* support weight loss in a way that reduces psychological strain
* avoid turning the process into constant under-eating and restriction

Calories matter most.
Protein matters secondarily.
Precise macro optimization is not a core focus.

The project is not trying to optimize for bodybuilding-style nutritional precision. It is trying to optimize for sustainable fat loss in real life.

---

## Position on movement

Low-intensity physical activity, especially walking, is an important part of the project's philosophy.

The project favors walking because it is:

* simple
* accessible
* repeatable
* low friction
* easier to sustain than more demanding interventions

Walking is not the headline of the project, but it is part of the method.

The general philosophy is that progress should come from a livable combination of:

* sustainable intake
* flexible food choices
* consistent deficit
* low-friction movement

Not from making life progressively smaller and harsher.

---

## What this project is not

This project is not:

* a generic calorie tracker
* a macro-optimization tool
* a meal logging app
* a clean-eating app
* a keto / carnivore / food-rules system
* a supplement-driven health optimizer
* a bodybuilding diet planner
* a punishment-and-recovery system
* a weight-loss product that assumes perfect conditions

It should never drift into those directions.

---

## What matters most

If future work has to choose between two options, prefer the one that improves these:

### 1. Adherence

Will this help the user stay with the process longer?

### 2. Friction reduction

Will this remove effort, complexity, or mental burden?

### 3. Flexibility

Will this help the system survive real-life variability?

### 4. Psychological sustainability

Will this make the process feel more livable and less brittle?

### 5. Weekly consistency

Will this help preserve direction across the week rather than obsess over single meals or days?

These are the core filters.

---

## What matters less

The following are secondary or non-essential at this stage:

* micronutrient optimization
* perfect nutritional database precision
* exact macro distribution
* feature completeness
* advanced analytics dashboards
* complex habit systems
* general health optimization beyond the weight-loss goal
* making the product look like a traditional fitness app

These should not distract from the core mission.

---

## Product-shaping implications

Even though this document is not a product spec, it does imply some important constraints.

### The system should feel guided

The user should not be left alone to figure everything out.

### The system should adapt quickly

If life changes, the plan should adapt without drama.

### The system should reduce manual tracking

Tracking should not become the center of the experience.

### The system should treat off-plan moments as normal

The right response is adjustment, not punishment.

### The system should work around the user's actual life

The user's preferences, routines, cravings, and context are not noise. They are the operating reality.

---

## What success looks like

This project succeeds if it helps users:

* stay consistent for longer
* stop restarting every few weeks
* lose weight without feeling constantly restricted
* include desired foods without losing control
* handle restaurants, cravings, and social meals without spiraling
* stop treating a single imperfect meal as failure
* build a way of eating that can actually last

The long-term promise is not just weight loss.

It is weight loss that does not fall apart the moment life gets messy.

---

## What is still intentionally undefined

This document does not define:

* the final product interface
* whether the first version is chat, Telegram, app, or something else
* the exact output format of plans
* the exact adjustment flows
* the exact data model
* the exact measurement or logging mechanics
* detailed product requirements

Those are product design questions for the next stage.

This document exists to lock the project direction before those decisions are made.

---

## Canonical project doctrine

If future work starts drifting, come back to this:

This project is about helping people lose weight through a sustainable calorie deficit with as much adherence and as little friction as possible.

It is built for people who fail restrictive diets in real life.

It is planning-first, flexibility-first, and anti-restriction.

It should handle cravings, restaurants, family meals, imperfect days, and real-life variability without collapsing into guilt, punishment, or restart cycles.

It is not another tracker.
It is not another diet with more rules.
It is a system designed to make weight loss actually livable.
